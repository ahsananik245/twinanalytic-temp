IoT Integration & Telemetry

Digital Twin Integration

Fusing Software with Architecture

Our Digital Twin Integration services bridge the gap between physical execution and digital asset management. We embed IoT strain gauges, temperature sensors, accelerometers, and tilt vectors into critical load-bearing concrete and steel elements during construction. These feed continuous, live telemetry data directly back to a high-fidelity 3D model replica of the building structure.

By comparing live stresses against original finite element limits, our software detects structural deviations early. This allows asset operators to identify load anomalies, schedule preventive maintenance, and audit the structure's performance over decades.

Our Execution Methodology

01

IoT Gateway & Address Registration

Deploying robust edge micro-servers and mapping communication address registers directly to coordinates within the BIM model basepoint.

02

Sensor Node Embedment

Embedding high-precision strain gauges, localized thermal monitors, and accelerometers within structural concrete pores prior to pouring.

03

MQTT Telemetry Calibration

Configuring MQTT data packets over encrypted enterprise bands to stream real-time telemetry inputs with less than 100ms latency.

04

Predictive Lifecycle Diagnosis

Running cloud model analytics to compare live sensor signals against finite element capacity designs to flag immediate maintenance alerts.

"Integrating Autodesk Tandem and AWS IoT Core, our digital twin dashboard at the Orion Tower maps real-time structural vibration rates, ensuring immediate deflection audits during seismic and wind occurrences."

Core Workflows & Deliverables

  • IoT Telemetry Mapping: Syncing physical stress and vibration data points with model components.
  • Automated Alarms: Configurable stress alarms that send immediate push notifications when limits are exceeded.
  • Predictive Diagnostics: AI models analyzing stress patterns to schedule maintenance.
  • Operations Dashboard: Cloud-hosted portal integrating spatial models with building management databases.

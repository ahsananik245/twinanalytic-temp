MEP Engineering

Sizing Building Systems

Our Mechanical, Electrical, and Plumbing (MEP) division works to design building environments that are energy-efficient and conflict-free. We model complicated duct routing networks, electrical conduits, and piping systems, checking them against structural steel frames to ensure simple installation field workflows.

Using computational fluid dynamics (CFD), we simulate thermal airflow distributions throughout the building envelope. This guides the placement of HVAC diffusers, optimizing air conditioning and heating cycles to secure smart energy targets.

Our Execution Methodology

01

CFD Thermal Airflow Sizing

Running ANSYS Fluent fluid dynamics loops to verify HVAC airflow profiles and thermal distributions across occupied rooms.

02

Electrical Smart-Grid Mapping

Sizing layout matrices for smart lighting grids, solar collector arrays, and distribution panel loops connected to primary backups.

03

Duct Collision Clearances

Routing complex HVAC lines, sewage pipes, and cable ladders, leaving 50mm clearances around all load-bearing structural columns.

04

Acoustic Sizing Audits

Testing noise vibration rates for large mechanical fans and detailing isolation pads to damp acoustics.

"Fusing Revit MEP layouts with structural constraints at the Solaris Research Complex, we resolved 1,200 mechanical clashes during design, optimizing HVAC power loads by 30%."

Core Workflows & Deliverables

  • CFD Airflow Simulations: Heat maps detailing thermal comfort and air change parameters.
  • Smart Grid Layouts: Electric diagrams mapping solar panels, backup power, and lighting paths.
  • Plumbing Conflict Checks: Coordinates resolution for storm and waste water routing.
  • Acoustic Insulation Audits: Sizing dampers to minimize HVAC noise transmission.
